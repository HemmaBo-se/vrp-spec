### What this means

Under OWFa 1.0 (Patent Only), §2.1 ("The Promise"), the Promisor irrevocably
promises **not to assert** any Granted Claims against any party for their
Permitted Uses of the Specification, and (§2.2) commits to a no-charge,
royalty-free license to those Granted Claims on reasonable and non-discriminatory
terms. "Granted Claims" are only those patent claims the Promisor owns or
controls that are **necessarily infringed by implementing the Specification**;
they do **not** extend to claims infringed only by adding features the
Specification does not require. "Permitted Uses" include making, using, selling,
and distributing a conforming implementation.

In plain terms: **anyone may implement VRP without seeking a patent license from
the Promisor.** This is a non-assertion covenant, not a transfer of ownership.
The standard OWFa termination provisions apply (e.g. the promise may be withdrawn
as to a party that brings a patent suit asserting that a Permitted Use infringes
its claims — the customary defensive-termination clause).
